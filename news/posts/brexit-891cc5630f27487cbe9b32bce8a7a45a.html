  1. Sovereignty and Control: Many Brexit supporters wanted to restore national control over laws, borders, and governance. They felt the EU imposed regulations and policies that limited UK autonomy, such as directives from unelected EU officials in Brussels.
  2. Immigration Concerns: A key issue was the desire to control immigration. EU free movement rules allowed citizens from member states to live and work in the UK, which some voters believed strained public services, housing, and wages.   3. Economic Independence: Some argued the UK could thrive outside the EU by negotiating its own trade deals globally, free from EU regulations and tariffs. Critics of EU membership highlighted contributions to the EU budget (around £8-9 billion net annually) as a drain on resources.
  4. Bureaucracy and Regulation: The EU was often seen as overly bureaucratic, with complex rules stifling UK businesses, particularly small and medium-sized enterprises. Brexit advocates believed leaving would allow the UK to tailor regulations to its needs.
  5. Cultural and National Identity: A sense of British exceptionalism and skepticism toward European integration fueled Brexit. Some voters felt EU membership eroded national identity, favoring a return to a more independent UK with stronger ties to the Commonwealth or English-speaking nations.
  6. Distrust in EU Institutions: Euroskepticism, longstanding in parts of the UK, grew from perceptions of the EU as undemocratic or mismanaged. Events like the Eurozone crisis and migration challenges in 2015 amplified doubts about the EU’s effectiveness.
  7. Political Discontent: Brexit was partly a protest vote against the political establishment. Many felt ignored by elites in London and Brussels, using the referendum to express frustration over issues like inequality, globalization, and austerity.
  8. Media and Campaign Influence: Pro-Brexit campaigns, like Vote Leave, emphasized emotive issues like sovereignty and immigration, amplified by media outlets. Claims like the £350 million weekly EU contribution (later debunked as misleading) swayed public opinion.
