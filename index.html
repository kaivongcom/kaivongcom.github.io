How to Activate Greyscale Filters:

  • iPhone/iPad: Go to Settings > Accessibility > Display & Text Size > Color Filters, then toggle on and select "Grayscale". A shortcut can be created by setting it to a triple-click of the side/home button.
  • Android: Navigate to Settings > Digital Wellbeing & Parental Controls > Bedtime Mode, or search for "Color Correction" in Accessibility to toggle Grayscale.
  • Windows 10/11: Press Windows logo key + U to open Accessibility settings, select "Color filters," turn them on, and select "Grayscale".
  • CSS (Web Design): Use filter: grayscale(100%); to apply the effect to images or entire webpages.

Benefits and Uses:

  • Digital Wellbeing: Reducing colors on screen decreases the brain's stimulation, making apps less addictive and reducing screen time.
  • Accessibility: Helps users with color blindness view content more easily.
  • Reduced Eye Strain: Can make reading and viewing text easier on the eyes.
